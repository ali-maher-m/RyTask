<ComingSoon tier="MVP scope" />

Notification overload is the most common complaint about work trackers, and the planned
answer is granular, findable preferences: choose what reaches you per project and per
event type, rather than a single all-or-nothing switch.

One control already works today: you can **snooze individual notifications** in the
inbox, and they re-surface later. The preference layer described here — deciding what
arrives in the first place — is the part that does not exist yet.

What's planned:

- **Per-project and per-event preferences** — granular opt-in to what notifies you (MVP scope).
- **Digest scheduling** — daily or weekly summaries instead of per-event noise (v2).
- **Mobile push** — phone alerts, once mobile apps exist (v2).
- **Global mute rules** — broad quiet controls across the workspace (v2).
